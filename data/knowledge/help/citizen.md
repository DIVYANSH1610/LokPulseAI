# Citizen Guide

Category: citizen

To report an issue, go to the Submit page. You can describe the problem
in your own words, upload a photo (the platform reads the issue and, when
available, the photo's location automatically), and set your ward. As you
type, the platform checks in the background whether someone nearby already
reported something similar — if so, you'll see an option to add your
voice to that existing report instead of filing a new one. Upvoting is
faster and strengthens the case for that issue's priority just as much as
a fresh report would.

The Browse page lets you look at issues already reported near you without
starting a new submission — useful if you just want to add support to
something you've also experienced.

The Status page shows every report or upvote you've made and its current
stage: new, under review, recommended, actioned, or resolved. There's no
login required — your submissions are tied to your device/session so you
can check back anytime.

If your report doesn't show a photo-based location, you can still tag your
ward manually during submission — this doesn't affect how seriously it's
treated.
